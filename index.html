Work for the semester:

This semester I want to start working on the acquisition of actual "employable" skills - which means deepening and expanding the knowledge we have acquired in our classes, as well as applying it to some proper projects. I picked the areas that I consider to be maintaining their relevance - like Data analysis, Cybersecurity, Databases and Natural language processing (NLP). Moreover, I wanted to focus on projects that will help develop our creativity and flexibility (such as this website) that can make us distinguishable on the market.

In this day and age, where everybody uses AI to code, I think it's worthwhile to step back and teach yourself the basics, however "inefficient" that may seem. Because at the end of the day, what you have gained is not only an experience, but also understanding. You cannot ask good questions (or put some into a prompter) if you don't know what to ask it in the first place. Garbage in, garbage out goes not only for training data but also for the users who put in prompts. So, in this semester, let's train our brains and make some stuff from scratch instead and get a better understanding of how stuff works through hands-on experience.
